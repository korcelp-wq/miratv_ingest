# Callosum Vector Integration (CVI)
## Foundational Parameters & Invariants

> **Status:** Foundational Specification  
> **Scope:** Industry / Infrastructure  
> **Intent:** Define coordination primitives, not application behavior

---

## 1. Purpose

Callosum Vector Integration (CVI) defines a **governed coordination fabric** for systems, databases, AI agents, and humans to exchange structured intelligence **without direct coupling**.

CVI does **not** define business logic, optimization goals, or decision outcomes.  
It defines **how intelligence is expressed, mediated, authorized, and recorded**.

---

## 2. Core Principles (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Coordination over Control**  
   CVI coordinates autonomous components; it does not centralize logic.

2. **Authority is Explicit**  
   Observation, synthesis, authorization, and execution are distinct roles.

3. **Append-Only Truth**  
   All communication is represented as immutable records.

4. **Mediated Execution**  
   No component executes actions without explicit authorization.

5. **SQL as Expressive Substrate**  
   SQL is used for deterministic aggregation and perspective formation, not free-form control.

6. **Transport Neutrality**  
   Records are tables; tables are interfaces.

---

## 3. Cognitive Stages

CVI recognizes four cognitive stages.  
These stages are **conceptual**, not implementation-specific.

| Stage | Description | Authority |
|------|------------|----------|
| Impulse | Signal + embedded reasoning | None |
| Idea | Stabilized synthesis | Advisory |
| Action | Authorized execution request | Authoritative |
| Outcome | Factual result | System |

No component may skip a stage.

---

## 4. Roles & Responsibilities

### 4.1 Observer
- Emits impulses
- Detects change
- Does not interpret significance

### 4.2 Synthesizer
- Aggregates impulses
- Produces ideas
- Does not execute

### 4.3 Authorizer
- Reviews proposals
- Approves or rejects execution
- Represents human agency

### 4.4 Executor
- Executes declared capabilities
- Reports outcomes
- Does not reinterpret intent

### 4.5 Historian (Knowledge Lake)
- Stores immutable records
- Preserves perspective over time
- Does not participate in coordination

---

## 5. Records (Conceptual)

> “Documents” are **structured records**, not files.

### 5.1 Impulse Record
- Emitted by systems or databases
- Contains:
  - signal
  - questions
  - provisional answers
  - uncertainty
  - provenance
- May be wrong
- May be ignored
- May not execute

### 5.2 Idea Record
- Derived from stabilized impulses
- Represents a coherent hypothesis
- Stored permanently in Knowledge Lake
- Advisory only

### 5.3 Action Request Record
- Authored only by authorized authority
- Names executable capability
- Requires explicit approval
- Triggers execution

### 5.4 Outcome Record
- Reflects actual execution result
- Immutable
- Closes causal loop

---

## 6. Conversations

- A conversation is a **set of related records** sharing a correlation key.
- Conversations are inferred by querying, not tracked by state.
- Conversations conclude when an Idea or Outcome is derived.
- No component may initiate a new conversation with a target while another is unresolved.

---

## 7. Consumption Semantics

A record is considered **consumed** only when the receiving component:

1. Ingests the record  
2. Evaluates it against expectations  
3. Either:
   - Executes a declared routine, or
   - Explicitly rejects the request  
4. Emits a response record

Consumption is proven by **response**, not mutation.

---

## 8. Knowledge Lake

The Knowledge Lake is the system of record for:

- impulses (raw memory)
- ideas (stabilized insight)
- actions (intent)
- outcomes (reality)

It does not:
- request data
- execute logic
- coordinate systems

Perspective emerges via query, not orchestration.

---

## 9. Execution Model

- All execution occurs via **declared stored procedures**
- No free-form commands
- No dynamic SQL from coordination layer
- Capabilities are owned by their home systems

CVI may request execution; it may not define execution.

---

## 10. Non-Goals (Explicit)

CVI does **not** define:

- Optimization heuristics
- Promotion thresholds
- Scheduling cadence
- Business semantics
- Domain vocabulary
- AI decision logic

These are policy or application concerns layered **on top** of CVI.

---

## 11. Invariant Summary

> **Signals may be noisy.  
> Ideas may be wrong.  
> Actions must be authorized.  
> Outcomes must be recorded.  
> Authority must be attributable.**

---

## 12. Classification

This specification is:
- Domain-agnostic
- Industry-portable
- Infrastructure-level
- Application-neutral

All implementations should treat this document as **normative** unless explicitly marked otherwise.
